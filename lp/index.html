Does retail market-making actually pay?

A paper-only test of whether a $250 maker captures any material edge from Polymarket's liquidity-rewards program — measured honestly against live book depth and adverse selection.

Independent research (24 of 25 claims verified) says retail market-making on Polymarket is net-unprofitable today: rewards are whale-dominated and informed traders pick off stale quotes. This dashboard doesn't try to beat that finding — it measures it. The prediction going in: rewards ≈ adverse-selection bleed, no material edge.

Verdict · measurement test

Rewards ≈ bleed. No material edge — exactly as the research predicted.

Over 23 paper days a $250 two-sided maker collected +$14.62 in liquidity rewards and gave back −$16.01 to adverse selection, netting −$1.39 (−0.56%). The reward stream is real but thin at this size — share never crosses ~0.4% of any pool — and informed flow reclaims it. The hard real-money gate stays closed: a successful experiment, because it priced the trade before risking a dollar.
